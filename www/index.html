Building a Blockchain for the Future of DeFi

In just a few years, Quantum Computing will become a genuine security risk for modern blockchains. The widely used encryption standard in current blockchains, asymmetric cryptography, will be vulnerable to attacks because of the advanced computational capabilities of quantum computers. QWID was developed to safeguard against this danger.

QWID utilizes a post-quantum cryptographic standard, which is mathematically demonstrated to withstand attacks from sophisticated quantum computers. The innovative dual-chain structure provides a decentralized PQC network without sacrificing security, scalability, or transaction speed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about QWID, post-quantum cryptography, staking, and the network.

QWID Technology
What is the QWID?
QWID is the world's first truly post-quantum blockchain, resistant to quantum computer attacks. It uses Proof-of-Synergy consensus and dual-blockchain architecture to address scalability issues inherent in post-quantum cryptography.
Why does QWID have two blockchains?
Post-quantum cryptography requires large public keys. Implementing this into networks like Ethereum would reduce block validation rates by 14x. The solution divides QWID into a primary chain for transactions and a secondary chain for storing public keys.
Does QWID support smart contracts?
Yes. QWID hosts a quantum-resistant decentralized virtual machine supporting smart contracts. QWID's virtual machine is EVM-compatible with Ethereum technology, enabling developer migration and new contract development.
What is the QWID RAND Oracle?
The RAND Oracle generates verifiably random numbers within smart contracts, replacing expensive third-party oracles. It provides per-block random numbers aggregated from network nodes at no cost for DeFi and GameFi applications.
What is the QWID PRICE Oracle?
The PRICE Oracle provides decentralized access to QWD token USD exchange rates within smart contracts. Network incentives encourage nodes to submit pricing data, with the median price determining the rate. Included per-block on the main chain at no charge.
Quantum Computing & PQC
What does PQC mean?
PQC means Post-Quantum Cryptography — cryptography that is resistant to quantum computer algorithms like Shor's or Grover's. QWID uses NIST-approved post-quantum standards to ensure long-term security.
What is Shor's algorithm and how does it break current encryption?
Shor's algorithm is a quantum computer algorithm for finding the prime factors of an integer, developed by mathematician Peter Shor. All standard non-PQC asymmetric cryptography used in Bitcoin and Ethereum can be broken by this algorithm with sufficient qubits.
What encryption standard does QWID use?
QWID leverages dual post-quantum signatures — Falcon-512 and MAYO-5 — both selected from NIST post-quantum cryptography finalists. Every transaction is signed with both algorithms, providing dual-layer protection with security equivalent to 128-bit AES encryption.
QWID Coin & Tokenomics
What is the maximum supply of QWD?
The total supply of QWD coin is 230,000,000 QWD. It is deflationary with a fixed total supply like Bitcoin. Block rewards diminish roughly once per day by a factor of 0.999.
How much QWD is premined prior to mainnet launch?
The mineable coin supply is 207,000,000 QWD. Pre-mined coins equal 10% of total supply: 23,000,000 QWD.
How are block rewards calculated?
Block reward = 1317.70833 × 0.999n where n equals the rough number of days from inception (one day is about 8,640 blocks). Rewards distribute proportionally to each staker's contribution.
Staking & Validation
What is the Proof-of-Synergy consensus model?
A consensus mechanism defining how distributed nodes reach agreement about which transactions appear on the chain. QWID combines elements of Proof-of-Work, Proof-of-Stake, and Proof-of-Authority for environmental friendliness, DDoS resistance, and decentralization.
What are delegated accounts?
256 delegate accounts are created when the blockchain is initiated. Those interested in staking deposit QWD to these accounts. The top 128 delegate accounts in terms of QWD holding act as nodes and validators.
What is the minimum stake I can delegate?
Users can deposit QWD in any of the 256 delegated accounts, as long as they deposit a minimum of 1,000 QWD. Withdrawals can occur anytime without fixed deposit periods. Deposits and withdrawals require 36 confirmations (~6 minutes) to become valid.
How many QWD do I need to operate my own node?
You need 1,000,000 QWD to create and operate your own node. You can also operate a node with less if other stakers join and collectively reach the threshold. Node operators set fees ranging from 0–50% of node rewards to cover operational expenses.
Are validators rewarded for validating blocks?
Only users staking coins to delegated accounts (nodes) that create valid blocks receive rewards, proportional to their staked amounts. The maximum number of reward-receiving nodes is limited to 128.
